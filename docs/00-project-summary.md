# 00. Project Summary

## Working Title

Tower Brawlers: A Soup-to-Nuts War

## One-Line Pitch

A silly side-view strategy game where two absurd kingdoms wage all-out war over completely unimportant nonsense by building towers, summoning weird troops, and yelling at each other until somebody's Very Important Tower falls over.

## Short Pitch

Tower Brawlers is a parody fantasy lane-war game inspired by Battle Towers. Players command one of two ridiculous factions across a single battlefield lane, balancing economy, troop deployment, tower building, and timed attack phases. The core strategy is real, but the world, dialogue, factions, and mission context are deliberately goofy.

## Genre

- lane-based strategy
- tower warfare
- campaign tactics
- mobile-first RTS-lite

## Tone

- comedic
- parody fantasy
- exaggerated and theatrical
- no serious war drama
- characters act like everything is historic even when the actual problem is stupid

## Core Fantasy

The player feels like a battlefield mastermind in a world full of idiots.

The ideal feeling is:

- "These people are all ridiculous."
- "My strategy is still clever."
- "I cannot believe this entire battle started because of soup."

## Core Player Promise

The game promises:

- short but dramatic battles
- readable strategy
- funny factions and units
- progression through unlocks and weird upgrades
- matches with real tactical decisions inside a light, accessible structure

## Main Design Pillars

### Readable Chaos

The battlefield should always be easy to read even when things are getting loud and silly.

### Tactical Timing

Players should win because they spend, build, and attack at the right moment.

### Petty Motivation, Epic Delivery

The writing treats tiny disagreements like grand historical disasters.

### Silly Surface, Solid Strategy

The jokes should support the game, not replace good systems.

### Fast Sessions, Strong Replay Value

Battles should be short enough for mobile sessions but varied enough to invite repeat play.

## Product Goals

- Build a strong single-lane strategy loop.
- Make the parody tone memorable from the first minute.
- Keep controls simple on touch screens.
- Allow long-term depth through units, items, structures, and mission modifiers.

## Core Match Goal

Destroy the enemy's main tower before they destroy yours.

## Match Length Target

- Tutorial missions: 3 to 5 minutes
- Standard missions: 4 to 8 minutes
- Boss missions: 6 to 10 minutes

## Content Scope for MVP

- 2 factions
- 1 full tutorial level
- 5 to 6 total missions
- 10 player troops
- 10 to 12 enemy units
- 8 to 10 structures
- 6 to 8 active items or abilities
- 1 boss fortress encounter

## Why This Concept Works

The gameplay foundation is proven. The parody framing gives it a stronger identity. Instead of trying to be another serious fantasy war game, TowerDefense becomes a comedic strategy game where the systems are tight and the characters are complete clowns.
