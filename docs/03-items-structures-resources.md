# 03. Items, Structures, and Resources

## System Goal

This document defines the economic and utility layer of the game: resources, collectible items, consumables, structures, and battlefield objects.

The tone stays parody-first, but the mechanics must remain strategically useful.

## 1. Core Resources

### Snacks

Primary in-match currency.

Used for:

- troops
- structures
- rebuilding
- some upgrades

Flavor:

Every army in Tallania claims morale comes from honor, but actual battlefield performance clearly depends on snacks.

### Drama

Secondary battlefield meter.

Used for:

- active faction abilities
- spells
- emergency buffs

Flavor:

Drama rises whenever something unfair, loud, or personally insulting happens.

### Coins

Meta-progression currency outside battle.

Used for:

- unit unlocks
- structure upgrades
- chapter progression
- cosmetic extras

## 2. Resource Rules

- Snacks generate passively.
- Economy buildings increase Snack generation.
- Drama builds from time, combat, damage, and losses.
- Coins are earned from mission completion and optional objectives.
- Resource names should always be shown with icons for clarity.

## 3. Consumable Items

Consumable items are optional pre-battle equips or mission rewards that create variety without overwhelming the player.

## 3.1 Starter Items

### Golden Soup Ladle

- Type: trophy item
- Effect: +5 percent starting Drama in the next mission
- Flavor: "Officially ceremonial. Unofficially pointy."

### Pocket Croutons

- Type: consumable
- Effect: gain a small burst of Snacks at match start
- Flavor: "Dry, stale, and surprisingly motivational."

### Emergency Napkin

- Type: consumable
- Effect: tower takes reduced damage for a short duration once per match
- Flavor: "Absorbs soup, tears, and minor siege debris."

### Whistle of Unnecessary Authority

- Type: consumable
- Effect: first troop deployed gains bonus movement speed
- Flavor: "Sounds official. Means nothing."

## 3.2 Midgame Items

### Banner Polish Kit

- Type: support item
- Effect: nearby allied troops gain tiny morale regen around player tower

### Spicy Mystery Stew

- Type: risk-reward consumable
- Effect: deployed troops gain bonus attack but may take self-damage over time

### Grubproof Boots

- Type: defensive item
- Effect: reduced enemy slow effects

## 4. Permanent Equipment Slots

Recommended equipment slots for campaign progression:

- 1 trophy slot
- 1 snack slot
- 1 utility slot

This keeps pre-battle strategy light and readable.

## 5. Structure Categories

### 5.1 Economy Structures

These improve resource flow and create strategic greed.

#### Snack Shack

- Faction: Sunnybottom Empire
- Purpose: increases Snack income
- Visual: striped booth with shiny pots and official signage

#### Slop Bucket Stand

- Faction: Mudsnout Horde
- Purpose: increases Snack income
- Visual: crooked stand, bubbling bucket, suspicious fumes

#### Coupon Cannon

- Neutral late-game structure
- Purpose: periodic burst of Snacks instead of steady income
- Visual: a machine that fires discount slips no one understands

### 5.2 Defensive Structures

#### Teacup Turret

- Faction: Sunnybottom Empire
- Purpose: ranged defense
- Style: polished ceramic launcher with dramatic recoil

#### Bone Barricade

- Faction: Mudsnout Horde
- Purpose: lane block and stall tool
- Style: ugly, practical, and somehow screaming

#### Complaint Bell

- Neutral utility defense
- Purpose: briefly stuns nearby enemies when activated
- Style: giant brass bell rung in moral outrage

### 5.3 Utility Structures

#### Praise Podium

- Faction: Sunnybottom Empire
- Purpose: buffs nearby allied troops
- Style: tiny stage with banners and speech scrolls

#### Yell Drum

- Faction: Mudsnout Horde
- Purpose: burst speed or aggression aura
- Style: giant drum played with reckless enthusiasm

#### Gravy Geyser

- Neutral hazard structure
- Purpose: area denial and slow effect
- Style: piping hot culinary disaster

## 6. Structure Design Rules

- Economy structures must be rewarding but punishable.
- Defensive structures should buy time, not create permanent stalemates.
- Utility structures should enable interesting tactics, not mandatory combos.
- Each structure needs a clear silhouette and visible damage state.

## 7. Battlefield Pickups

Pickups are temporary map objects or mission rewards inside battles.

### Snack Crate

- grants instant Snacks
- encourages short power spikes

### Megaphone

- grants bonus Drama
- supports comeback moments

### Official Stamp

- temporary tower armor boost
- presented as absurd bureaucracy

## 8. Upgrade Paths

Structures and items should have branching upgrades.

Example:

### Snack Shack Upgrade Paths

- Bigger Portions: more steady income
- Rush Delivery: faster first payout
- Greasy Efficiency: lower build cost

### Teacup Turret Upgrade Paths

- Extra Saucer: more durability
- Stronger Brew: more damage
- Splatter Shot: mild area effect

## 9. Economy Balancing Rules

- Early economy should feel tempting.
- Greedy economy openings must be punishable.
- Comebacks should still be possible through Drama abilities and good timing.
- Resource starvation should feel like a consequence, not random cruelty.

## 10. Naming Rule

All item and structure names should be mechanically readable first and funny second. Players should understand function from name or icon without reading a paragraph.
