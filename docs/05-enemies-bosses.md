# 05. Enemies and Bosses

## Purpose

This document defines enemy-facing units, rival commanders, elite encounters, and boss concepts.

In campaign terms, "enemy" means whichever faction the player is not currently controlling, plus special neutral threats and chapter bosses.

## 1. Enemy Design Goals

- enemies should be readable quickly
- each enemy wave should teach or test something
- rival commanders should feel funny and memorable
- bosses should be mechanically clear and narratively ridiculous

## 2. Basic Enemy Types

### Rush Enemy

Fast, weak, pressure-focused.

Purpose:

- forces player reaction
- punishes greed

Examples:

- Tunnel Nibbler
- Royal Overreactor

### Tank Enemy

Durable and slow.

Purpose:

- creates frontline anchoring
- allows ranged support to matter

Examples:

- Spoon Guard
- Big Auntie Rumblegut

### Siege Enemy

Threatens towers directly.

Purpose:

- forces player to respond before lane collapse

Examples:

- Crumpet Catapult
- Sludge Lobber

### Support Enemy

Increases efficiency of surrounding enemies.

Purpose:

- teaches target priority

Examples:

- Trumpet Medic
- Gobble Shaman

## 3. Rival Commanders

Rival commanders appear in mission intros, taunt during combat, and define enemy doctrine.

## 3.1 Lady Marmalade von Pomp

- Faction: Sunnybottom Empire
- Role: theatrical fortress commander
- Personality: speaks in official announcements even during combat
- Battle gimmick: buffs towers and summons defenders when insulted

Sample line:

"This siege is unauthorized and, frankly, unsightly."

## 3.2 Captain Sogtooth

- Faction: Mudsnout Horde
- Role: ambush commander
- Personality: smug, loud, and delighted by bad plans
- Battle gimmick: surprise rush waves and sabotage structures

Sample line:

"If the tower falls crooked, that still counts!"

## 3.3 Inspector Grandwell

- Neutral committee boss
- Role: rules-obsessed bureaucrat
- Personality: thinks forms can stop warfare
- Battle gimmick: pauses the fight with absurd rule checks and penalties

Sample line:

"Please stop fighting until section twelve is initialed."

## 4. Elite Enemy Concepts

### Complaint Cavalry

- Empire elite
- charges into the lane after issuing paperwork
- punishes weak frontlines

### Cauldron Hurlers

- Horde elite
- area-damage artillery
- punishes clustered troops

### Compliance Drone

- Committee elite
- floating stamp machine that debuffs structures
- useful in parody bureaucracy missions

## 5. Boss Battle Philosophy

Bosses should not just be giant health bars. Each boss battle needs:

- one obvious gimmick
- one escalation phase
- clear telegraphing
- strong personality
- visually absurd presentation

## 6. Boss Concepts

## 6.1 The Monument of Excessive Glory

- Faction: Sunnybottom Empire
- Type: fortress boss
- Theme: an overdecorated tower with too many attachments

Mechanics:

- rotating side cannons
- morale pulse buff to defenders
- decorative mirrors that reflect projectiles during one phase

Comedy angle:

The tower literally has statues of itself attached to itself.

## 6.2 The Grand Slop Citadel

- Faction: Mudsnout Horde
- Type: fortress boss
- Theme: huge crooked fort made of wood, bone, and confidence

Mechanics:

- spits sludge pools into the lane
- releases cheap swarm units periodically
- enters tantrum mode at low health

Comedy angle:

Parts of the building appear to have been added mid-battle.

## 6.3 The Hall of Official Decisions

- Faction: Neutral committee
- Type: special chapter boss
- Theme: a moving paperwork fortress

Mechanics:

- pauses deployment with "review periods"
- summons stamp drones
- creates zones that require unit permits

Comedy angle:

The final phase starts because someone forgot to sign the emergency war exception form.

## 7. Level 1 Enemy Details

Enemy faction in Level 1: Mudsnout Horde

Enemies used:

- Mud Grunter
- Fork Flinger
- Slop Bucket Stand

Mini-boss presence:

- none

Narrative pressure:

The enemy does not yet feel threatening in a dark sense. They feel noisy, rude, and unexpectedly effective.

## 8. Enemy Scaling Plan

### Early Game

- simple waves
- obvious counters
- small unit count

### Mid Game

- mixed unit roles
- enemy support behavior
- early siege pressure

### Late Game

- elite units
- boss mechanics
- map-specific wave rules
- doctrine-style enemy behavior

## 9. Enemy Readability Rules

- support enemies need distinct shapes
- siege enemies need obvious setup animations
- fast enemies need clear movement language
- bosses must communicate phase changes visually and audibly
